# PRD: Michelson Interferometer Research-Grade Simulab ## 1. Project Overview A dual-purpose web application designed for both undergraduate education and high-precision PhD-level research simulation of a Michelson Interferometer. The system utilizes Three.js for 3D visualization, WebAssembly (Wasm) for high-precision physics calculations, and WebGPU/Compute shaders for real-time interference pattern rendering. ## 2. Technical Architecture - **Rendering Engine:** Three.js with custom GLSL shaders for volumetric laser propagation and real-time fringe rendering. - **Physics Core:** C++/Rust compiled to Wasm for 64-bit precision (picometer scale). - **Computation:** Gaussian beam propagation models, Jones Calculus for polarization, and stochastic noise modeling (Wiener processes). - **UI/UX:** State-driven "Toggle Architecture" to switch between Pedagogical and Research modes. ## 3. Key Research Features - **Gaussian Beam Profiling:** Full modeling of beam waist, Rayleigh range, and Gouy phase shifts. - **Noise Modeling:** - Phase Noise (laser linewidth jitter). - Environmental Noise (seismic/acoustic vibration). - Detector Noise (Shot noise, Dark current, Quantum Efficiency). - Thermal Expansion (Invar vs. Aluminum mounts). - **Advanced Modules:** - Quantum Panel: Squeezed vacuum state injection. - Astronomical Panel: Gravitational wave strain (LIGO-scale simulation). - **Precise Controls:** Microradian tip/tilt, picometer translation, and material-specific thermal coefficients. ## 4. User Experience & Flows - **Interactive 3D Setup:** Drag-and-drop optical components on a 25mm grid breadboard. - **Real-time Feedback:** Immediate fringe pattern updates on the detector screen as variables change. - **Data Export:** CSV and TIFF export of simulated detector arrays for external analysis (Python/MATLAB). - **Simulab Presets:** Pre-configured setups for HeNe, Nd:YAG, and Gravitational Wave detection scenarios. ## 5. Visual Standards - High-fidelity GLTF models of kinematic mounts. - Volumetric raymarching for laser beams. - Physically accurate wavelength-to-color mapping.
